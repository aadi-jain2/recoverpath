Real model performance

How the risk score is actually generated.

The RecoverPath risk score comes from a two-layer model: four Isolation Forests each specialize in one signal domain (cardiac, respiratory, fluid, activity) and feed their anomaly scores into an XGBoost classifier alongside 44 wearable features, trained to predict 30-day readmission risk from a 24-hour sensing window.

Architecture: Isolation Forest ×4 + XGBoost Test set: 12,000 windows (3,867 positive) Task: 30-day readmission risk

This model is deliberately tuned as a screening tool, not a diagnostic one. At its evaluated threshold it catches 94.1% of true readmissions (sensitivity) — but it also flags roughly half of stable patients as a false positive (specificity 49.3%). That's a real tradeoff, not a rounding error: RecoverPath is built to prioritize not missing a deteriorating patient over keeping the false-alarm rate low, on the assumption that a clinician reviewing a flagged case costs far less than a missed decompensation. Trained primarily on synthetic wearable data — real-world clinical validation is the next step before this feeds a live workflow.

What that means in practice

At the evaluated operating threshold, the model is tuned toward catching true deterioration even at the cost of a high false-positive rate — the numbers below make that tradeoff explicit rather than leading with the flattering ones.

94.1%Sensitivity (recall) — share of true readmissions caught
49.3%Specificity — share of stable patients correctly cleared
46.9%Precision — share of flags that were true readmissions
0.626F1 score at this threshold

Top model drivers

What actually moves the risk score.

Ranked by SHAP feature importance from the trained model — the same sensor modalities introduced earlier on the main site, confirmed by the data rather than asserted.

  1. 01 Days with atrial fibrillation detectedLonger AFib exposure in the window is the single strongest driver of predicted risk. ECG · MAX30001
  2. 02 Share of ECG readings showing AFibHow much of the monitoring window had arrhythmia present, not just whether it occurred once. ECG · MAX30001
  3. 03 Time spent with blood oxygen below 92%Cumulative hypoxia exposure — a direct read on lungs filling with fluid. SpO2 · MAX30102
  4. 04 Variability in blood oxygen readingsUnstable SpO2, not just a low average, flags respiratory decline. SpO2 · MAX30102
  5. 05 Total coughs detected in 24 hoursRising cough frequency is an early acoustic signature of respiratory deterioration. Acoustic · MAX4466
  6. 06 Diabetes diagnosis on recordComorbidity context the model weighs alongside the live sensor signal. Clinical history
  7. 07 Hospital admissions in the last 90 daysRecent admission history is one of the strongest non-sensor risk predictors in readmission research generally. Clinical history
  8. 08 Share of hours with wheeze detectedSustained wheeze across the window, not an isolated event. Acoustic · MAX4466
  9. 09 COPD diagnosis on recordComorbidity context, same role as the diabetes flag above. Clinical history
  10. 10 Variability in breathing rate from motion sensingIrregular respiratory rate, derived from chest-strap motion, flags labored breathing. Activity · MPU-6050

Known limitation: this model was trained primarily on a clinically-grounded synthetic dataset (with the UCI Diabetic Readmission dataset for demographic features and a small PhysioNet PPG sample for reference), not on real patient outcomes from RecoverPath hardware. The numbers above describe how well the model performs on its own held-out synthetic test split — they are not yet clinical validation, and shouldn't be read as one.
